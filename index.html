Louisville Stunt Lot

About

Welcome to Louisville Stunt Lot! With any luck, you are here because you have a motorcycle that you like to stunt. Or, at least, you like stunts. We offer a safe, legal space for stunters to practice and show off their tricks. We also offer a concessions stand stocked with all the snacks you could desire; as well as some essential parts for the expected breakdown. With EMTs on staff and Stunt Supervisors refereeing the fun, you can't find a better option! For a detailed breakdown of the Rules and Requirements, check here.

Rules & Requirements

For your convenience, we've broken this into several sections!

Gear

You MUST Have:

  • A DOT approved helmet.
  • Full fingered gloves
  • A long sleeved/thick top. Motorcyle jackets preferred, hoodies permitted. Other tops to be evaluated by Stunt Supervisors upon entry.
  • Long pants
  • Closed toe shoes

Vehicle

Your vehicle must have the following:

  • Two Wheels. As in ONLY two wheels
  • Functioning brakes
  • NO loose wires
  • NO leaks of any kind
  • NO loose parts/bolts/etc.
  • Head & tail lights
  • Your vehicle does not need to be "street legal" if it is trailered in.

On The Track

For everyone's safety, some rules exist while you are on the track. This is not a definitive list. If you're not certain if a particular maneuver is permitted, ask the Stunt Suprvisor on duty. The Stunt Supervisor will always have the final say for safety. Any rider that does not comply with the directions of the Stunt Supervisor may be ejected.

Some General Rules:

  • OBEY the Flags!
    • Green starts the time session.
    • Yellow is typically directed at one rider as warning for risky behavior.
    • Red is full stop. All riders, all bikes, stop. Listen to the Stunt Supervisor and the lot will re-open quickly!
    • Checkered flag marks the end of the session. Follow the directions of the Stunt Supervisor and exit the course!
  • Saw a rider go down? You went down? Activate the strobe light puck attached to your bike! Do NOT cross in front of moving vehicles to reach the down rider. Stay put.
  • Do NOT intimidate other riders on the track. Such behaviors could be riding too closely, swerving in front of, not yielding head on, etc.
  • Keep it friendly. No one is an expert. No rude commentary on failed or shaky stunts
  • DON'T be a space hog! No, not the band. A literal space hog. Don't be that guy. Yield some room and play nice.
  • Generally any stunts that involve contact between two riders (such a toe touches) need to be coordinated with the Stunt Supervisor first. Additonally, the Stunt Supervisor may veto the stunt if the lot is considered too crowded.

Spectators

Spectators are fully welcome and should feel free to hit up our concessions stand for cold drinks and snacks! NO spectators are authorized inside the track area for any reason. Spectators are held to same applicable rules as riders and will be asked to leave the premises if they cannot comply. Otherwise, bring a lawn chair (provided seating is often limited), sit back, and cheer on your favorite stunts.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Can I ride without a helmet?
    • Absolutely not. Any attempt to do so will be denied
  • Are there gift cards available?
    • Sure are! See the concessions/booth table for purchasing one.
  • I bought more time than I needed, can I split it up?
    • Of course! You don't have to run all your paid sessions back to back. You will receive a tear ticket for each 30 min increment. Convert any extra time into a gift card at the end of the night.
  • I want to ride stunts but I'm a beginner, am I welcome?
    • Yes! We regularly host beginner only sessions and sometimes a guided class if an instructor is available.
  • Do you carry spare clutch/brake levers?
    • We carry some universals but it is HIGHLY recommended you carry one spare of each.
  • I'm big on burnouts, do you have tires and tire tools?
    • The bigger events have more tires/options and staffing to actually assist you. Some common sizes are carried in a limited selection on regular nights. Staffing should also be treated as limited on regular nights.
